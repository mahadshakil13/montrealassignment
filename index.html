Welcome to Little France, Montreal

Montreal is the second most populated city in Canada, and is located in the Provice of Quebec. It has a long and rich history with France reaching back to the 16th century. French Canadians are the majority in Montreal, which is why it is referred to as Little France. I do not know any French despite being born from there, buit I plan on learning french when I get the chance. Montreal is the biggest host of international events in North America, and is home to the Cirque de Soleil, the largest contemporary circus in the world! Montreal has so many other amazing attractions to see and things to do!

Popular Attractions

  • Vieux-Montréal

    • Old Montreal is home to many buildings from the 17th, 18th and 19th centuries, showing off the architecture from when Montreal was still a young town.

  • Vieux-Port

    • The old Port is located right next to Old Montreal, making it a perfect place to visit next. It is located by the Saint Lawrence River, and the port houses a Ferris wheel, and an IMAX theater as well!

  • Botanical Garden

    • Located in Parc Maisonneuve, the Botanical Garden in Montreal is one of the best you can visit, with over 30 themed gardens and 10 exhibition greenhouses, there is a wide variety of plants with different applications and colors.

  • St. Joseph's Oratory

    • The oratory is located near Mount Royalk Park, and is dedicated to Canada's patron saint, St. Joseph. It was originally a small chapel, where Brother Andre performed miraculous acts of healing, and was later converted to an Oratory.
